Intorduction

F1, short for Formula 1, is the highest class of international single-seater motor racing. Governed by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA), F1 is famous for its cutting-edge technology, precision, and speed. Drivers compete in Grand Prix races held across the globe, from Monaco to Melbourne, in state-of-the-art cars capable of reaching speeds over 300 km/h (186 mph).

History

Formula One originated from the World Manufacturers' Championship (1925–1930) and European Drivers' Championship (1931–1939). The formula is a set of rules that all participants' cars must follow. Formula One was a formula agreed upon in 1946 to officially become effective in 1947. The first Grand Prix in accordance with the new regulations was the 1946 Turin Grand Prix, anticipating the formula's official start. Before World War II, a number of Grand Prix racing organisations made suggestions for a new championship to replace the European Championship, but due to the suspension of racing during the conflict, the new International Formula for cars did not become formalised until 1946, to become effective in 1947.
